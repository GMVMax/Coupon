How it works

① Coupons unlock extra shoppers
Coupons convert price-sensitive shoppers who'd otherwise bounce. Bigger r → bigger lift, with diminishing returns. Ceiling = U (Audience Upside Cap).
② How fast the curve climbs
S = curve speed. High S → tiny coupons move the needle. Low S → need bigger coupons.
③ Profit has a sweet spot
GMV saturates; coupon cost grows linearly. The 🏆 marks the rate where profit peaks.
Where is the extra GMV coming from?
Ad budget and ROI target don't change — who converts does. Without a coupon, only non-price-sensitive shoppers convert. With one, the same ad $ also captures the price-sensitive segment, so effective ROI overshoots the floor (5.0× → up to 5.0×(1+U)). U = size of that price-sensitive segment.
Equation: GMV(r) = GMV0 × [ 1 + U × (1 − e−S·r) ] · Coupon cost = r × GMV · Profit = GMV × GM − Ad Spend − Coupon cost.
